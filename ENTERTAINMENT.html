Toni Fowler drops latest song "Pribado" to educate kids about protecting their bodies

Published on November 1, 2025 · By ABS-CBN News | SBB Entertainment Desk

Content Warning: This article contains mention of sexual harassment and assault.

Social media influencer and singer Toni Fowler just dropped her latest single, “Pribado,” last October 31. The FPJ's Batang Quiapo actress' latest song aims to educate children about their right to protect their private parts — a bold and empowering move to promote awareness about personal boundaries.

Toni's latest song is a collaboration with Vince Flores and Papi Galang. The inspiration came from a heartfelt discussion on her podcast “Fowler's Position”, where they talked about their childhood experiences and the importance of consent.

“Kaya namin inilabas tong kanta na ito dahil dito sa video na 'to,” she explained in the comment section.

The song's lyrics speak frankly about private body parts, using real anatomical terms to remove shame and promote education. It reminds listeners that consent and respect must always come first.

“Ito ay pribado, sensitibong usapan, dapat seryosohin at hindi dapat pagtawanan... Ang labi ay pribado, wag basta pahalikan sa kung sino-sino. Bawal yan, bawal yan.”

The song also emphasizes the importance of reporting any form of harassment: “Mali yan, mali yan. Wag mo akong hawakan. Isusumbong kita kay nanay at kay tatay. Pribadong parte ng katawan iniingatan.”

Netizens applauded Toni, Vince, and Papi for using music as a tool to protect children and spread awareness. Many parents praised the initiative for opening up conversations that are often avoided at home and in schools.

One commenter wrote, “This should really be shown in schools so students understand that not everything should be allowed, especially when it's about our bodies.” Another added, “Some might laugh or make fun of this video, but the message behind it is no joke. It's a call to protect every child's right to safety and respect.”

The music video, uploaded on Toni's official YouTube channel, has already surpassed half a million views — and counting. With its honest message and educational intent, “Pribado” proves that music can be more than entertainment; it can be a powerful platform for change.
