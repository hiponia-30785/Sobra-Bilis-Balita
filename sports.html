Green shines as Suns thump Clippers

Published on November 7, 2025 · By ABS-CBN News | SBB Sports Desk

Jalen Green made a triumphant season debut for the Phoenix Suns on Thursday, scoring 29 points in a dominant 115-102 NBA victory over the short-handed Los Angeles Clippers.

"It felt good to be back," a beaming Green said in an on-court interview. "I was telling everybody on my team, I was like I couldn't even take my nap fully I was so anxious to get back on the floor.

The Suns, trailing by three after a tight first half in which the lead changed hands 11 times, exploded with 40 points in the third quarter, making their first eight shots of the period and pushing their lead to as many as 20.

Devin Booker added 24 points as the Suns, a disappointing 0-4 on the road, improved to 4-1 at home.

Ivica Zubac led the Clippers with 23 points and 11 rebounds, but the absence of Los Angeles' two top scorers, Kawhi Leonard and James Harden, was too much to overcome.

Boxer Leo Mhar Lobrido eyes golden comeback after Asian Youth Games bronze

Leo Mhar Lobrido, who comes from the proud Vicera boxing family that has produced Olympians, puts his loss in the Asian Youth Games in Bahrain into perspective

Lobrido is eyeing a golden comeback after settling for bronze in the Asian Youth Games following a unanimous decision loss to Uzbekistan's Abdugani Yorkinjonov in the semifinals of the boys' 46kg division at the Exhibition World Bahrain here on Tuesday, October 28.

Lobrido dominated Jordan's Salte Al Hadidi in the preliminary, then beat Sri Lanka's Binul Dulnada Dewasiri Narayana in the quarterfinals to guarantee himself a medal.

“The loss means that I still have to do more training and apply the things I lack. Maybe next time I'll win gold,” said Lobrido, who also served as the Philippines' flag bearer in the opening ceremony together with volleybelle Harlyn Serneche.


Football

Cristiano Ronaldo has revealed he plans to retire “soon” as the Portugal and Al Nassr star prepares for a tearful end to his glittering career.

The 40-year-old, who has scored a remarkable 952 goals, is one of the most decorated players in the history of the game, but the end is in sight for his astonishing career.

Asked when he would consider hanging up his boots, Ronaldo, who is under contract with Al Nassr until 2027, replied: “Soon. But I think I will be prepared.

“Nothing will be able to compare that the adrenaline that we have for football to score a goal,” he said.

“But everything has a beginning, and everything has an end. I have other passions. I'm going to have more time for me, I'm going to have more time for my family to raise my kids.
